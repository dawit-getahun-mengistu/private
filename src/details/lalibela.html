Lalibela

Lalibela is a town located in the Amhara region of Ethiopia, known for its rock-cut churches. The town is home to 11 medieval monolithic churches, which were carved out of solid rock and are considered some of the most impressive examples of Ethiopian architecture. Lalibela is a popular tourist destination and was declared a World Heritage site by UNESCO in 1978.

Important Phone numbers in Ethiopia

How To Get To Lalibela

To get to Lalibela, you can travel by car, bus, or plane. The town is located about 1,000 kilometers north of Addis Ababa and can be reached by driving, taking a bus, or flying to the nearest airport in Axum and then taking a taxi or hiring a car. It is advisable to plan your trip in advance and book your transportation accordingly.
