Aksum

Aksum is an ancient city located in northern Ethiopia. It was the capital of the Kingdom of Aksum, which flourished between the 1st and 7th centuries AD and was a major trading center in the region. Aksum is known for its impressive ruins, including the obelisks of Aksum, which are tall, carved stone pillars that were erected as monuments. The city is also home to the Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion, which is said to contain the Ark of the Covenant. Aksum is a popular tourist destination due to its rich history and cultural significance. It was declared a World Heritage site by UNESCO in 1980.

How To Get To Aksum

To get to Aksum, you can travel by car, bus, or plane. The city is located about 740 kilometers north of Addis Ababa and can be reached by driving, taking a bus, or flying to the nearest airport in Axum and then taking a taxi or hiring a car. It is advisable to plan your trip in advance and book your transportation accordingly.
